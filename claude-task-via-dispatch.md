HOW TO GIVE CLAUDE EFFECTIVE INSTRUCTIONS (via Dispatch)
=========================================================

These steps are based on the kind of task we just did together:
opening a browser, navigating to an app, and saving a screenshot.

---

1. SPECIFY THE APP OR URL
   Tell Claude exactly where to go.
   Example: "Open Chrome and go to localhost:5000"
   Example: "Navigate to https://myapp.com/dashboard"

2. STATE THE ACTION CLEARLY
   Be direct about what you want done.
   Example: "Log in" / "Click the Submit button" / "Take a screenshot"
   Avoid vague phrases like "do something with it" or "handle it"

3. NAME YOUR OUTPUT FILE (if saving anything)
   Tell Claude the exact filename and where to save it.
   Example: "Save the screenshot to my Desktop as ClaudeTest"
   Example: "Create a PDF report in my Documents folder named Report_March"

4. CHAIN STEPS IN ORDER
   If the task has multiple steps, list them in sequence.
   Example:
     - Open Chrome and go to localhost:5000
     - Log in
     - Take a screenshot
     - Save it to Desktop as ClaudeTest

5. GIVE CONTEXT WHEN NEEDED
   If Claude might not know what an app is or what to look for, add a short note.
   Example: "It's a printer management app, I want to see the printers list"

6. CONFIRM SENSITIVE ACTIONS YOURSELF
   Claude will always ask before submitting forms, making purchases,
   or changing account settings. Just reply "yes" or "confirm" to proceed.

7. USE DISPATCH FROM YOUR PHONE
   You can send all of the above from the Claude mobile app (Dispatch tab)
   and Claude will carry out the task on your desktop computer.

---

EXAMPLE OF A WELL-STRUCTURED INSTRUCTION:
"Open Chrome, go to localhost:5000, log in with the saved credentials,
navigate to the Printers page, take a screenshot, and save it to
my Desktop as PrinterStatus_Today."

---

TIPS:
- The more specific you are, the less back-and-forth needed.
- You can ask Claude to describe what it sees before taking action.
- Claude will always confirm before doing anything irreversible.
